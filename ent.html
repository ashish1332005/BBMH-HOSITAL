Symptoms and Diseases of ENT

  • Ear Disorders : Hearing loss, Clogged ear, Ear pain, Ear noise, Nerve pain, Facial and Cranial nerve disorders, Balance disorders, Tinnitus
  • Nose : Smell disorders, Nasal obstruction, Running nose, Sinusitis, Polyps, Appearance disorders, Sleep apnea, Septum, Swallowing disorders, Problem in the larynx.
  • Throat Disorders : Throat problems like tonsillitis, difficulty swallowing, and voice issues

Tympanoplasty

Tympanoplasty is a surgical procedure to repair a perforated eardrum and improve hearing. It restores ear function and prevents infections. This procedure is particularly beneficial for individuals who have suffered from chronic ear infections or trauma to the eardrum. If left untreated, a perforated eardrum can lead to hearing loss and increased vulnerability to infections, making tympanoplasty a crucial treatment option for such cases. The surgery is performed using microsurgical techniques, ensuring precision and high success rates.

FESS (Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery)

FESS is a minimally invasive procedure to treat chronic sinusitis and nasal polyps. It removes blockages, improves breathing, and reduces infection risks. Patients who experience persistent sinus infections, facial pain, or nasal congestion can benefit significantly from this procedure. By using an endoscope, doctors can precisely target and remove obstructions without major incisions, resulting in quicker recovery times and minimal post-operative discomfort.

Thyroidectomy

Thyroidectomy is the removal of all or part of the thyroid gland, treating conditions such as thyroid cancer, goiter, or hyperthyroidism. The thyroid gland plays a crucial role in regulating metabolism, and any abnormalities in its function can significantly impact overall health. Thyroidectomy is recommended when medication or other treatments fail to control thyroid disorders, ensuring long-term relief and prevention of complications.

Head and Neck Cancer Surgery

Head and neck cancer surgery involves removing tumors from the mouth, throat, or voice box. Early diagnosis and treatment improve survival rates. This type of surgery is often combined with radiation or chemotherapy for comprehensive cancer management. Patients undergoing head and neck cancer surgery may also require reconstructive procedures to restore normal appearance and functionality, ensuring the best possible quality of life post-surgery.
